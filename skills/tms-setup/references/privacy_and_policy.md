# Privacy And Policy Boundaries

TMS OS can support planning without student-identifiable data.

## Treat As Sensitive

- Student names.
- Student emails.
- Rosters.
- Grades.
- Attendance.
- Behavior notes.
- IEP, 504, medical, counseling, or family information.
- Raw Google Forms responses tied to identifiable students.
- Uploaded student work, including photos, screenshots, videos, audio, and files.
- Media-release status.

## Default Rule

If the teacher has not supplied an approved data-use process, do not store raw identifiable student information in the project.

Use:

- Aggregate patterns.
- De-identified labels.
- Completion counts.
- Common misconceptions.
- Readiness splits.
- Teacher-approved summaries.

## Allowed Before Policy Is Known

- Lesson planning.
- Opening boards.
- Handouts.
- Rubrics.
- Exit-ticket question design.
- Live Google Form creation when the project stores only metadata and links.
- Google Forms-ready specs.
- Class-memory notes that avoid names and grades.

## Requires Teacher Approval Or Known Policy

- AI analysis of raw identifiable responses.
- Pulling examples from identifiable student responses.
- Reading uploaded student images, video, audio, or files.
- Saving response exports into the project.
- Student-specific intervention lists.
- Grade status summaries.
- Accommodation-based grouping.
- Parent/contact recommendations.
- Public publishing workflows involving student media.

## Response Analysis Default

When the teacher asks to analyze a Google Form or linked Sheet, prefer:

1. Completion count.
2. Common misconception.
3. Readiness split.
4. Next instructional move.
5. De-identified examples only when explicitly approved.

Do not paste raw rows, student emails, names, or uploaded student files into chat or project files unless the teacher supplied an approved storage and data-use process.

## Final Wording

When policy is missing, say:

```text
I can continue planning and artifact creation, but I should not process student-identifiable evidence beyond aggregate patterns until you provide the approved data-use boundary.
```
