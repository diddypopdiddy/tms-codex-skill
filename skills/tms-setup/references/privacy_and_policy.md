# Privacy And Policy Boundaries

TMS OS can support planning without student-identifiable data.

## Treat As Sensitive

- Student names.
- Student emails.
- Rosters.
- Grades.
- Attendance.
- Behavior notes.
- IEP, 504, medical, counseling, or family information.
- Raw Google Forms responses tied to identifiable students.
- Media-release status.

## Default Rule

If the teacher has not supplied an approved data-use process, do not store raw identifiable student information in the project.

Use:

- Aggregate patterns.
- De-identified labels.
- Completion counts.
- Common misconceptions.
- Teacher-approved summaries.

## Allowed Before Policy Is Known

- Lesson planning.
- Opening boards.
- Handouts.
- Rubrics.
- Exit-ticket question design.
- Google Forms-ready specs without live student response analysis.
- Class-memory notes that avoid names and grades.

## Block Until Policy Is Known

- Student-specific intervention lists.
- Grade status summaries.
- Accommodation-based grouping.
- Parent/contact recommendations.
- AI analysis of raw identifiable responses.
- Public publishing workflows involving student media.

## Final Wording

When policy is missing, say:

```text
I can continue planning and artifact creation, but I should not process student-identifiable evidence until you provide the approved data-use boundary.
```
